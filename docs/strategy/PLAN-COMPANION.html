Stellaris — ZK Proof-of-Reserves on Stellar

An issuer proves on-chain that sum(reserves) ≥ liabilities without revealing any account balance. A Soroban contract verifies the Groth16 proof and records a non-replayable “Solvent as of ledger N” attestation.

Circom + Groth16 Soroban verifier ZK load-bearing ~7-day window $10k pool / 5 places Deadline 2026-06-29

Why this wins

Best feasibility-to-differentiation ratio for a 7-day build: a single circuit + the official verifier, ZK that can’t be removed, chain-native demand (issuers are Stellar’s real customers), and a field thin on finished proof-of-reserves projects.

The gate it passes

Delete the proof and the product is a trust-me PDF. The contract refuses to record solvency unless a valid Groth16 proof verifies on-chain via BLS12-381 pairing host functions. The proof is the trust primitive.

The 15-second differentiator

On camera, lower a balance below liabilities: the proof still generates, but the contract rejects with NotSolvent. That is the most convincing proof that the ZK is real.

Honest limitation (stated everywhere)

The proof binds the arithmetic and non-negativity of the committed reserve vector — not the truthfulness of the issuer’s raw balance inputs. Binding raw balances to reality needs a custodian/exchange oracle, explicitly out of scope. Everything here is a research prototype, not audited. This is the standard, well-understood boundary of every proof-of-reserves system; stating it plainly builds trust with judges.
